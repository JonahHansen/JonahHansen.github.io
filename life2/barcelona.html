Visiting Barcelona

Welcome to Barcelona! We know your primary focus during your stay is the LIFE conference, but we also encourage you to take some time to experience the vibrant culture and unique charm of this incredible city. Even with a packed schedule, there are many fantastic activities you can fit in to make the most of your visit. Here are a few suggestions that are easily accessible and offer a great overview of Barcelona.

Modernist Route

No visit to Barcelona is complete without witnessing the architectural Modernist style and the masterpieces of its genius, Antoni Gaudí. Beyond the famous Sagrada Familia, consider exploring the "Quadrat d'Or" (Golden Square) in the Eixample district. Here you'll find other masterpieces like Casa Batlló and Casa Milà (La Pedrera) on Passeig de Gràcia, easily admired as you stroll along this elegant avenue. You can not miss the Parc Güell, a complex of gardens and architectural elements inspired by nature. It is within walking distance of the conference venue!

Old Town

Experience the lively atmosphere of Les Rambles, Barcelona's most famous boulevard. While it can be bustling, it's an essential part of the city's character. Just off Las Ramblas, you'll find the Mercat de la Boqueria, a vibrant food market perfect for a quick, delicious bite.

Just take some of the streets adjacent to Las Ramblas to wander through the narrow, winding streets of the Gothic Quarter. This historic heart of the city is home to the Barcelona Cathedral, charming squares, and hidden alleyways. It's perfect for a leisurely walk, enjoying a coffee, or discovering local artisan shops.

Adjacent to the Gothic Quarter, the trendy El Born neighborhood offers a mix of medieval charm and modern boutiques, bars, and restaurants. It's also home to the stunning Santa Maria del Mar Basilica and the Picasso Museum.

Panoramic Views

You can have some nice panoramic views of the city from top of the two famous Barcelona mountains: Montjuïc and Tibidado.

Take a cable car or funicular up to Montjuïc mountain from the city center. Here, you'll find a historic castle, beautiful gardens, and several museums including the National Art Museum of Catalonia (MNAC). The views of the city and the harbor from the top are absolutely spectacular.

For the most expansive panoramic views, head to Tibidabo mountain. It's home to an amusement park, the oldest one in Spain dating back from 1901, and the stunning Sagrat Cor Church. The journey up with the funicular, the stop is just 10 minutes walking from the conference venue, offers increasingly impressive vistas, making it a great option for an afternoon excursion.

Alternatively, you can go up to one of the tallest buildings in the city, the Glòries tower. You will enjoy a 360 deg panoramic view. You can also enjoy the skyline of the city from the sea shore on board one of the typical boats in Barcelona, “Las Golondrinas”. A different way to view the city. Some useful links:

Museums

Barcelona boasts a fantastic array of museums catering to all interests. Here are some examples:

  • CosmoCaixa: The CosmoCaixa Barcelona, the conference venue, is an interactive science museum that allows visitors of all ages to engage with scientific principles through hands-on exhibits and immersive experiences. Highlights include a stunning recreation of a "Flooded Forest" depicting an Amazonian ecosystem, a fascinating Geological Wall, and a Planetarium offering immersive journeys through the cosmos. Go and check the current temporal exhibition!
  • Museu Nacional d’Art de Catalunya (MNAC): The MNAC offers a vast collection of Catalan art, with a world-renowned display of Romanesque frescoes and murals alongside significant works spanning Gothic, Renaissance, Baroque, and Modern art periods. You'll also find excellent examples of photography and decorative arts, all within the stunning setting of the Palau Nacional with panoramic city views.
  • Museu d’Art Contemporani de Barcelona (MACBA): The MACBA is dedicated to contemporary art from the second half of the 20th century to the present day, showcasing a diverse collection of works by Catalan, Spanish, and international artists. Visitors can explore a wide range of artistic expressions, including painting, sculpture, video art, and photography, often focusing on critical dialogues about art, culture, and society.
  • Centre de Cultura Contemporània de Barcelona (CCCB): The CCCB is a dynamic cultural center that doesn't house a permanent collection, but instead offers a diverse program of temporary exhibitions, debates, and festivals focused on contemporary culture and urban issues. Visitors can explore a wide range of topics, often related to art, science, and society, presented through various media including film, photography, and interactive installations, all within an architecturally striking building that blends historic and modern elements.
  • MOCO Museum Barcelona: The MOCO Museum Barcelona focuses on inspiring modern, contemporary, and street art, featuring works by renowned artists like Banksy, Andy Warhol, and Jean-Michel Basquiat. Visitors can experience a unique collection that often includes thought-provoking digital and immersive art installations.
  • Museu Marítim de Barcelona (MMB): The Maritime Museum of Barcelona is housed in the historic Royal Shipyards (“Drassanes Reials”) and offers a comprehensive journey through Catalonia's rich maritime history. Visitors can explore an impressive collection of model ships, navigation instruments, cartography, and maritime artifacts, with a highlight being the full-scale replica of the "Galera Real" (Royal Galley) from the Battle of Lepanto.
  • Museu Picasso de Barcelona: The Museu Picasso in Barcelona offers an unparalleled insight into the formative years of Pablo Picasso, showcasing an extensive collection of his early works, including paintings, drawings, and ceramics. Visitors can particularly see his artistic development from childhood through his academic training and his iconic "Las Meninas" series, a fascinating reinterpretation of Velázquez's masterpiece.
  • Poble Espanyol: Located on Montjuïc, the “Poble Espanyol” (Spanish Village) is an architectural museum featuring reproductions of buildings, squares, and streets from different regions of Spain. It's a unique way to experience the diversity of Spanish architecture and culture in one place.

We hope you have a productive and enjoyable time at the conference, and that you also get a chance to experience the magic of Barcelona!
