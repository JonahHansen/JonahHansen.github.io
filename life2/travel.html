Travel to and around Barcelona

Arriving at Barcelona-El Prat airport

  1. Aerobús (A1/A2) to Plaça Catalunya:
    • Catch the Aerobús (blue bus) directly outside your terminal.
      • Terminal T1: follow the signs inside the airport.
      • Terminal T2: exit the main building and turn left. The bus stop is approximately 100 meters away.
    • The journey to Plaça Catalunya takes approximately 35 minutes.
    • Cost: A single ticket is €7.20, a return ticket is €12.85.
    • The ticket is valid for three months and can be scanned and presented to the driver directly from your smartphone.
  2. From Plaça Catalunya to CosmoCaixa (additional information in the Venue details section):
    • FGC Line L7: From Plaça Catalunya, take FGC Line L7 (brown line) towards Avinguda Tibidabo.
    • Av. Tibidabo Stop: Get off at the Av. Tibidabo stop (last stop of the line).
    • From Av. Tibidabo to CosmoCaixa:
      • Walk: Cosmo Caixa is approximately a 15-minute walk uphill from the Av. Tibidabo FGC station.
      • Bus 196: You can also take bus line 196 from near the Tibidabo FGC station directly to CosmoCaixa (same ticket).

Arriving at Barcelona Sants train station

  1. Metro from Sants Estació to Plaça Catalunya:
    • From Sants Estació, take Metro Line L3 (green line) towards Trinitat Nova.
    • Get off at Plaça Catalunya (3 stops).
  2. From Plaça Catalunya to CosmoCaixa:
    • Follow the same directions as above

General travel tips and public transportation in Barcelona

  • T-Casual card (recommended!): If you're staying for a few days, we recommend purchasing a T-Casual card.
    • This card offers 10 journeys for €12.15 (Zone 1, which includes out to Sant Cugat del Vallès; but note that Rubí and Sabadell are Zone 2).
    • It's valid on the metro, buses, and FGC trains. You can purchase it at metro and FGC stations.
    • You can also install the T-Mobilitat app on your smartphone (Android • iOS) to top up your virtual ticket. Then, simply tap your phone on the barriers to validate your ticket and begin your journey. This method works on the metro, FGC trains, and buses.
  • Alternative bus routes to CosmoCaixa:
    • Several bus lines have stops relatively close to CosmoCaixa, including H2, H4, V17, V15, V19, V13, 196, and 123. Use a mapping app for the most convenient route from your exact location. See below for more venue information.
  • Arriving by car:
    • Via Ronda de Dalt:
      • Use Exits 6 or 7 of the Ronda de Dalt. Follow signs towards Tibidabo/Sant Gervasi.
    • Parking at CosmoCaixa:
      • CosmoCaixa has its own underground parking facility, Saba - CosmoCaixa, located at Carrer dels Quatre Camins, 89.
      • It is open from 7:00 to 23:00.
      • Important Note on City Parking: Parking on the street in Barcelona can be challenging due to regulated "green" and "blue" zones with time limits and fees. Using a secured car park is generally recommended. You can also explore options for booking parking in advance through services like Parclick or ElParking.
  • Taxi/Ride-hailing services
    • The following companies operate in Barcelona: Uber, Bolt, Cabify, and FreeNow.
  • Bicycles and scooters
    • In Barcelona, you can rent bicycles and electric scooters. However, be aware that the latter are not permitted on the metro and FGC trains, and riding on the pavements is not allowed.
  • Tourist information points:
    • Airport Terminals 1 & 2.
    • Plaça Catalunya.
    • Metro L9 lobby at Barcelona's Airport Terminals.
    • Check the Barcelona Tourisme website for more information.
